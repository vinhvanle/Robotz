Group Relfection

What went well?

Generally, despite some minor conflicts, our team’s working process went extremely smoothly. Although it could be difficult to communicate and understand each other's opinions, as we have a foreigner member; everyone contributed to the project in different ways and that has given us a successful project in the end. There wasn’t a lot of conflict during the whole process, and even when there was one, we resolved the problem very quickly and in a very respectful and positive attitude. Since the team regularly talked to each other and gave helpful feedback, everyone’s part was very good, succinct and integrated.

Furthermore, the core idea of our project was brilliant thanks to the team’s creativity, dynamic and analytical thinking. When there’s a new idea, the team member listens to each other and contributes to that idea very well. Although most team members are a beginner of the IT field and his limited knowledge and skills, the team made a lot of efforts to comprehend the robot in-depth, instead of giving up.

What could be improved?

We believe one thing that our team could improve in the future is organizing more face-to-face meetings to speed up completing the project. As we also attended other courses, which our schedules did not really sync, organizing face-to-face meetings was a little bit difficult. Now, with more spare time in semester break, the team members could meet more frequently to continue building the robot as soon as possible.

Another problem that we experienced in the first two weeks of working on this project is member recruitment. Initially, our team only has 4 members and only until the 3rd week, we are able to welcome Lee Ga In to the team. This has resulted in serious time-consuming when it comes to finishing tasks, building prototypes and writing the report. Moreover, the process of re-allocating the roles and tasks was complicated due to this issue.

One thing that was surprising

An aspect that really surprised us when working together is the advanced knowledge and practical skills in Computer Science/Information Technology of the teammates. To be more specific, Van Vinh has a background in computer language, electrical engineering and some experience working with Arduino microcontroller; which was impressive to me for an Introduction to IT student. Moreover, Ga In was very skillful in using CSS computing language when designing the website. These CSIT knowledge and skills are extremely useful when it comes to increasing the practicality of the project idea and developing both hardware and software of the prototype.

On the other hand, as final-year Digital Marketing students, Mai Ly, Tuan Viet and Viet Anh have wide knowledge in marketing as well as the skills of using marketing tools. Thus, this is a major advantage for the team to deeply conduct market research and advertise this project in the future.

One thing we learn from working together

From working with this group, there are a large number of professional and soft skills that we are able to learn from each other, which could contribute to the employability of our team in the future. Specifically, Le Van Vinh gained new knowledge on marketing, presentation, as well as videography. Meanwhile, Lee Ga In learned effective presentation skills and knowledge about designing and assembly the robotics components. One thing that Mai Ly, Tuan Viet and Viet Anh learned about the group is some interesting knowledge in the field, for example the Arduino microcontroller, as well as good teamwork skills, particularly how to use tools like GitHub to collaborate with other software developers.
